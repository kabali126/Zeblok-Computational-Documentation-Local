## Overview

An **Ai-MicroService** is a containerized service that you can deploy on demand from the Ai-MicroCloud platform. This guide walks you through every field on the **New Microservice** form so you can configure and launch your service with confidence.

To get started, navigate to **Workspace → Microservices** in the left sidebar. If you have not spawned any microservices yet, you will see an empty state page. Click the **New Microservice** button in the top-right corner to begin.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Configure">

    The **Configure** tab is the first step of the setup form. It collects the core settings needed to define what your microservice runs and where it is hosted.

    ### Microservice *(required)*

    Use the **Microservice** dropdown to select the type of containerized service you want to deploy. Several public templates are available, including:

    - **nginx**
    - **test**
    - **openclaw agent**
    - **MongoDB**
    - **PostgreSQL**
    - **MySQL**
    - **MLflow**
    - **ChromaDB**
    - **Nginx**

    Click on the template that best matches your use case. Each option is labeled **Public**, indicating it is available to all users on the platform.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/microservice-select.png" alt="Microservice type selection dropdown showing available microservice templates" />
    </Frame>

    ---

    ### Image Tag *(required)*

    After selecting a microservice type, choose the **Image Tag** — the specific Docker image version that will be used to run your service. The tag marked **Latest** is the most recently published version and is recommended for most use cases.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/image-tag.png" alt="Image tag dropdown showing available tags including the Latest tag" />
    </Frame>

    ---

    ### Data Center *(required)*

    Select the **Data Center** where your microservice will be deployed. The dropdown lists all data centres available to your organisation. Each entry shows the data centre name, location, and whether it is the primary region.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/datacenter-select.png" alt="Data Center dropdown showing the Phison-Prod data centre in Redmond, WA, US" />
    </Frame>

    ---

    ### Plan *(required)*

    Choose a **Plan** to define the compute resources allocated to your microservice. Each plan displays the number of GPUs, vCPUs, RAM, and local disk storage it provides. Few plans are listed below:

    | Plan Name | GPU | vCPU | RAM | Disk |
    |---|---|---|---|---|
    | Custom-Plan-cQvtb7u9 | 0 | 2 | 3 GB | 0 GB |
    | Custom-Plan-_v1Yoesd | 0 | 1 | 4 GB | 0 GB |
    | test plan | 0 | 1 | 1 GB | 10 GB |
    | Custom-Plan-UdVCUEnq | 1 | 14 | 48 GB | 100 GB |
    | Custom-Plan-1lr3aae9 | 2 | 16 | 32 GB | 0 GB |

    Select the plan that matches your expected workload. If none of the existing plans meet your needs, click **+ New Dynamic Plan** to create a custom resource configuration.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/plan-select.png" alt="Plan selection dropdown listing available compute plans with GPU, vCPU, RAM, and disk details" />
    </Frame>

    ---

    ### Replicas

    Set the number of **Replicas** — the number of instances of your microservice that will run simultaneously. The default is `1`. Increasing replicas improves availability and distributes load across multiple instances.

    The following additional deployment options are also available on the Configure tab:

    - **Stateful** — enable this toggle if your microservice requires persistent storage tied to each replica. Stateful services retain their data across restarts.
    - **Spawn in different worker nodes** — distribute replicas across different physical worker nodes to improve fault tolerance.
    - **Spawn in different availability zones** — distribute replicas across different availability zones for higher resilience against zone-level failures.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/other-configure-options.png" alt="Replicas field and deployment toggles including Stateful, Spawn in different worker nodes, and Spawn in different availability zones" />
    </Frame>

    Once you have filled in all required fields on the Configure tab, click **Next** to proceed to the Environment tab.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Environment">

    The **Environment** tab collects deployment-level settings such as the namespace, service name, networking, environment variables, commands, and optional storage configuration.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/environment-tab.png" alt="Environment tab showing namespace, service name, protocol and ports, environment variables, arguments, command, volume path, and attach bucket fields" />
    </Frame>

    ### Namespace *(required)*

    Select the **Namespace** in which your microservice will be deployed. Namespaces are used to organise and isolate workloads within your organisation. Use the dropdown to choose from the namespaces available to your account.

    <Note>
      You must be a member of a namespace to deploy into it. If the namespace you need is not listed, contact your OrgAdmin to be added. OrgAdmins can manage namespace membership from the IAM section.
    </Note>

    ---

    ### Service Name *(required)*

    Enter a unique **Service Name** to identify your microservice. The default value is `microservice`. You can customise this to reflect the purpose of the service — for example, `postgres-db` or `ml-inference-api`.

    ---

    ### Protocol & Ports *(required)*

    This section defines how your microservice exposes its services. By default, a single entry is pre-configured:

    - **Protocol:** HTTP
    - **Port:** 80
    - **Label:** UI-default

    Port `80` is the standard HTTP port. You can click **Add Port** to expose additional ports if your microservice runs other services on different ports.

    ---

    ### Environment Variables *(optional)*

    Add key-value pairs that will be injected as environment variables into your microservice container at startup. This is useful for passing in configuration values, API keys, database connection strings, or runtime flags. Click **Add Env Variable** to add additional rows.

    ---

    ### Arguments *(optional)*

    Pass command-line arguments to the microservice's entrypoint process. Enter each argument in the provided field (for example, `--workers=4`). Click **Add Argument** to add additional argument rows.

    ---

    ### Command *(optional)*

    Override the default command passed to the microservice container. Enter each command on a separate line without single quotes. Leave this field blank to use the image's built-in `CMD` or `ENTRYPOINT`.

    ---

    ### Volume Path *(optional)*

    Enter a container path where a persistent volume should be mounted (for example, `/home`). This is useful if you want a specific directory inside the container to be backed by durable storage.

    ---

    ### Attach Bucket *(optional)*

    Use this setting to attach an object storage bucket to your microservice. The default value is **No**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/attach-bucket-yes-selection.png" alt="Attach Bucket dropdown showing the Yes option being selected" />
    </Frame>

    Set it to **Yes** to mount a bucket directly into the microservice's filesystem. When enabled, two additional fields appear:

    - **Bucket Name** — the name of the bucket to attach, auto-populated from your account's available buckets.
    - **Mount Path** — the path inside the container where the bucket will be accessible, for example `/home/abhinav/d7240f30e840-abhinav`.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/attach-bucket-after_selecting-yes.png" alt="Bucket Name and Mount Path fields shown after selecting Yes for Attach Bucket" />
    </Frame>

    Once all required fields are filled in, click **Create** to deploy your microservice.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Accessing Your Microservice">

    After clicking **Create**, the platform provisions your container and redirects you to the Microservices list. Your new microservice will appear here with its current status. Once the status shows as **Running**, it is ready to use.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/after-spawning-microservice-ready.png" alt="Microservices list showing the newly spawned microservice in a Running state" />
    </Frame>

    From the microservice card you can click **View Details** to open the full details panel, where several actions are available:

    ### Open the Microservice

    Click the **Open** button to launch your microservice's public endpoint directly in your browser. This takes you to the live URL where your service is accessible.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/click-open-to-open-microservice.png" alt="Clicking the Open button to access the microservice's live endpoint in the browser" />
    </Frame>

    ### View Details

    The **Details** tab shows the full runtime and configuration information for your microservice, including status, endpoint URL, cluster URL, data centre, plan, namespace, image tag, requested resources, ports, and volume path.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/click-details-for-all-info.png" alt="Details panel showing full microservice configuration including status, endpoint, plan, namespace, and resource details" />
    </Frame>

    ### View Logs

    Click the **Logs** tab to access the real-time container logs for your microservice. This is useful for debugging startup issues or monitoring running processes inside the container.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/click-logs.png" alt="Logs tab showing real-time container output for the microservice" />
    </Frame>

    ### Monitor Resource Usage

    Click the **Monitoring** tab to view live resource utilisation metrics for your microservice, including CPU usage, memory usage, GPU utilisation, CPU throttling, and disk I/O metrics such as read speed, write speed, read IOPS, and write IOPS. You can filter metrics by time range — 5 minutes, 30 minutes, 2 hours, 12 hours, or 24 hours.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/microservices/click-monitoring.png" alt="Monitoring tab showing CPU, memory, GPU, and disk I/O metrics for the microservice" />
    </Frame>

  </Step>

</Steps>

## Bring Your Own Microservice

The Ai-MicroCloud platform is not limited to the built-in public templates. If you have a proprietary service, a custom-built API, or a specialised containerized workload, you can import it directly into the platform and deploy it just like any other microservice.

Head over to **Platform → Imports → AI Model Hub** to onboard your own Docker image — complete with custom ports, private registry credentials, and your preferred compute plan.
